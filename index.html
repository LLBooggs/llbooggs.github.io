Building & Campus
Climate Action Priorities

The 1957 systems are in their replacement window. The decisions made in the next 12 months — about what equipment goes in and how the building envelope is treated — will shape All Souls' energy profile and emissions for the next 20 years.

This plan ranks 16 science-based actions by climate impact and feasibility for All Souls specifically. Ten are ready to start this year. Two more belong in the 2026–27 capital budget. Four are worthwhile upgrades to pursue when renovation cycles create natural openings.

Most of the immediate actions pay for themselves through utility savings. They are also, at their core, an expression of who we are: a congregation committed to the interconnected web of all life, and to meeting this moment with the same moral seriousness we bring to every justice challenge.

Improve Refrigerant Management
Buildings & Industry
Drawdown IPCC AR6 Ch.9 RMI
▶ Act Now
Audit all HVAC systems for HFC refrigerant leaks. HFCs are up to 9,000× more potent than CO₂. Fix leaks and plan for low-GWP alternative refrigerants at next equipment replacement.
A 1957 campus with aging HVAC almost certainly has leaking refrigerant. The fastest and cheapest climate action available to All Souls — schedule an audit immediately. With replacement planned, also ensure your HVAC contractor follows EPA Section 608 refrigerant recovery requirements during decommissioning — proper recovery is legally required and prevents HFC release into the atmosphere.

Use Heat Pumps
Buildings & Electricity
Drawdown RMI WRI IPCC AR6 Ch.9 DOE BTO ASHRAE Handbook
▶ Act Now
Replace gas or aging HVAC with all-electric heat pumps at end-of-life. Heat pumps provide both heating and cooling at 3× efficiency of direct resistance. Impact grows as Oklahoma's grid gets cleaner.
The HVAC replacement this year or next is the most important climate decision window currently open to All Souls. Specifying all-electric heat pumps — rather than conventional gas equipment — in this cycle eliminates combustion from the building and locks in high efficiency as Oklahoma's grid continues to decarbonize. Missing this window means 15–20 years of unnecessary emissions. Get heat pump contractor bids immediately alongside conventional bids for comparison. Tulsa's mild winters make heat pumps especially well-suited here.

Improve Building Envelopes
Buildings & Electricity
Drawdown IPCC AR6 Ch.9 RMI WRI DOE BTO ASHRAE 90.1
▶ Act Now
Add wall/roof insulation and air-sealing. Use low-embodied-carbon materials (cellulose, mineral wool) per Drawdown's "Alternative Insulation Materials" solution — also Highly Recommended at −$121/t. Do this before replacing HVAC to rightsize new systems. WRI: every $1 spent on efficiency avoids $2+ in energy supply investment.
With renovation funds in hand and HVAC replacement imminent, this is the right moment. Commission an energy audit immediately — heat load calculations before equipment is specified will rightsize the new HVAC system and could reduce equipment cost by 20–30%. Insulation and air-sealing bundled into the renovation scope earns the highest ROI. Doing envelope work after HVAC replacement means oversizing — wasted capital and higher operating cost for years.

Improve Windows & Glass
Buildings & Electricity
Drawdown IPCC AR6 Ch.9 RMI DOE BTO ASHRAE 90.1
▶ Act Now
Retrofit single-pane windows with double-glazing plus low-e coating. Windows drive 30–50% of heating/cooling loads. Low-e glass blocks solar heat gain — critical for Tulsa's intense summer sun. Global impact: equivalent to removing 455–565 million cars/yr (2.10–2.60 Gt CO₂-eq; 1 Gt = 1 billion metric tons).
With window replacement actively under consideration and renovation funds available, the decision window is open now. Low-e double-glazing on south- and west-facing glass is especially high-impact in Tulsa's intense summer sun, directly reducing cooling load on the new HVAC system. Specify low-e coatings and solar heat gain coefficients (SHGC ≤ 0.25 for south/west exposures) when getting bids. Phasing by wing is reasonable if budget requires it.

Automate Building Systems
Buildings & Electricity
Drawdown ASHRAE GL-13 DOE Better Buildings RMI WRI
▶ Act Now
Install smart thermostats, occupancy sensors, and a building management system (BMS) to match HVAC and lighting to actual use — not a fixed schedule.
All Souls' occupancy pattern — empty most of the week, with two high-use days (Sunday services and Wednesday Connections programs) — makes BMS exceptionally high-value. Can cut HVAC runtime 30–50% in unoccupied zones. 2–4 year payback at 42,000 sq ft. Coordinate BMS installation with the HVAC replacement — bundling smart controls into the HVAC contract is significantly cheaper than a standalone retrofit later.

Electricity

Rooftop solar PV generates on-site clean electricity, offsetting grid power. Tulsa receives ~5.5 peak sun hours/day. A 200 kW system on 42,000 sq ft could offset 30–50% of electricity use. IRA Sec. 48E provides a 30% direct cash payment to nonprofits — no tax liability needed. Projects must begin construction by July 4, 2026 to remain eligible.
Install after envelope + LED upgrades so you rightsize the array. The 30% IRA direct pay is a game-changer for nonprofits. A visible solar installation is also a powerful public statement of All Souls' values. If any roof work is planned as part of the renovation, this is the ideal time to complete a solar feasibility assessment and reserve roof space — installing after a fresh roof avoids future re-work.

Expand tree canopy and plantings around the building. Strategic shading on south and west exposures can cut cooling loads 20–30%. Also sequesters carbon and reduces stormwater runoff.
The 5-acre campus is an asset. Native plantings on the west and south faces of the sanctuary and Emerson Hall shade the building during Tulsa's brutal afternoon heat. Reduce Food Waste & Improve Diets NEW
Food, Agriculture, Land & Ocean
Drawdown IPCC AR6 SPM WRI Cool Food Interfaith P&L
▶ Act Now
Two of Drawdown's highest-impact solutions: Reduce Food Loss & Waste (≈ 270–540 million cars/yr equivalent; 1.23–2.47 Gt/yr) and Improve Diets — shifting toward plant-rich meals (≈ 305–610 million cars/yr; 1.40–2.80 Gt/yr — the single largest solution in the entire database).
Emerson Hall hosts coffee hours, potlucks, and community meals regularly. Shifting toward plant-forward menus and composting food scraps directly enacts two of Drawdown's top solutions — and aligns perfectly with UU values. Consider joining WRI's Cool Food Pledge (free for mission-driven nonprofits) — a verified commitment to 25% per-plate GHG reduction by 2030, with a free Coolfood Calculator to track progress across Sunday coffee hours and Wednesday Connections meals. 90+ institutions serving 2.8 billion meals annually have already committed; verified members have achieved an average 11% reduction so far.5
